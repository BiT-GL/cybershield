⚔️ 1. Phishing Attacks: The Digital Con Game

Phishing = pretending to be someone else to trick you into giving info.
How to beat it:
* Check the sender’s email address. Looks weird? Random numbers? Nope. Trash it.
* Don’t click suspicious links. Hover your mouse over links to see where they really go.
* Look for spelling mistakes or urgent scare tactics. “Your account will be deleted!!” Nah. That’s bait.
* Use 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) everywhere — even if they get your password, they won’t get in.
* Golden Rule: If in doubt, don’t click.

⚔️ 2. Malware: The Silent Assassin

Malware = bad software that sneaks into your device to steal, break, or spy.
How to beat it:
* Install trusted antivirus software. Even Windows Defender is solid these days.
* Keep software updated. Updates = fixing security holes.
* Never download random attachments or pirated software. Free isn’t always free.
* Use official websites only. Want VLC Player? Get it from videolan.org, not some sketchy clone site.
* Be careful with USB drives. Especially ones you “found.” That’s a hacker trap.

⚔️ 3. Weak Passwords: Leaving Your Door Unlocked

Weak password = hacker walks in like they own the place.
How to beat it:
* Use long, complex passwords. Example: GreenDog!7_OrangeMoon#2025
* Don’t reuse passwords. One site gets hacked, and you’re toast everywhere.
* Use a password manager. Bitwarden, LastPass, or 1Password — they remember the chaos so you don’t have to.
* Bonus trick: Make passphrases like sentences. IHave3Cats&1Dog! Easy to remember, hard to crack.
